Parts of the Violin:

learn a little about violin anatomy :

violin body: This is the body of the instrument. It is made out of maple, ebony and spruce! The body is usually held by the neck, and thats where players place thier fingers to make notes.
Chinrest and Endpin this is where the player rests their chin to play the instrument. the little circle piece at the bottom of the violin is the endpin. it is small, but very durable. It maintainins all the string tension the violin has when in tune and when played.
F- holes this is where the sound generated escapes the body of the instrument
tailpiece this piece does a lot. it assists in holding the tension for the strings and it is for tuning the violin, but only when minor tuning is needed.
tuning pegs this is where major violin tuning happens. if your string is out of tune by a lot, you will use these
Finger Board the fingerboard is where your fingers go to play notes. notice that there are no frets or markings on the fingerboard, so you have to use you ears to detrmine if you are in tune or not. It requires muscle memory
